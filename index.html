Eirspace — Head of Rocketry Structures

Leading rocket structure development, hardware integration, and testing for student-built launch systems.

Structures • Integration • Testing

As Head of Rocketry Structures at Eirspace, I led work on the structural systems of student-built rockets, with a focus on turning designs into manufacturable, testable, and reliable hardware. This involved coordinating interfaces between structures, avionics, recovery, and propulsion so that the rocket could be assembled and tested as a complete engineering system.

A major part of the role was hardware integration and validation. I worked through assembly constraints, fit checks, structural requirements, and practical issues that only appear when components are brought together. The work required balancing design intent with manufacturability, safety, reliability, and testing feedback.

This project developed my ability to lead technical work, communicate design decisions, and manage the gap between analysis and physical hardware. It also strengthened my interest in high-reliability engineering environments where verification and careful integration are central to success.

Formula Trinity — Vehicle Performance & Lap-Time Simulation

Modelling vehicle behaviour to support design decisions in a student motorsport environment.

Simulation • Dynamics • Engineering Analysis

Developed a lap-time simulation framework for Formula Trinity to estimate vehicle performance and support engineering decisions. The project focused on representing vehicle parameters, track conditions, acceleration, braking, and cornering behaviour in a way that could be used to compare design choices.

The work connected mechanical design with analysis and coding. Rather than treating the simulator as a standalone software task, I approached it as an engineering tool: identifying which assumptions mattered, what outputs were useful, and how modelling could guide practical vehicle-development decisions.

This project strengthened my ability to build simplified but useful engineering models, interpret simulation outputs critically, and communicate performance trade-offs clearly within a design team.

Muffin Dynamics — Pipe-Climbing Inspection Robot

Mechanical design, traction, packaging, and test-led iteration for an inspection system.

Mechanical Design • Prototyping • Testing

Developed a pipe-climbing inspection robot concept focused on mechanical packaging, traction, stability, and practical testing. The project required designing a system that could maintain contact with the pipe surface while carrying the required components and remaining compact enough for inspection use.

The main engineering challenge was balancing the mechanical design against real-world constraints: grip, normal force, component placement, assembly access, and reliability during movement. I treated the project as a design-and-validation problem, using prototypes and testing feedback to refine the mechanism rather than relying only on the initial CAD model.

This work strengthened my understanding of mechanical design iteration, especially the importance of testing early, identifying failure modes, and improving the physical design based on observed behaviour.

Autonomous Buggy — Integrated Engineering System

Combining mechanical design, controls, sensing, and testing into a functioning vehicle platform.

Systems Engineering • Controls • Testing

Built and developed an autonomous buggy platform involving mechanical assembly, sensing, control logic, and practical testing. While the project included software and autonomy, the main engineering value came from integrating the full system so that the vehicle could behave reliably under real operating conditions.

The project involved tuning control behaviour, debugging hardware and software interactions, and understanding how physical constraints affected performance. Work on the buggy reinforced the importance of designing around the complete system rather than treating mechanical, electrical, and software elements as separate problems.

This project gave me experience in practical engineering integration: identifying where system behaviour diverged from expectations, diagnosing problems through testing, and improving both the hardware setup and control response.
